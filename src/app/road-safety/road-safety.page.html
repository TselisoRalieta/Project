Going on a road trip?

Get a good night's sleep

Ensure at least 7–8 hours of rest before driving. Fatigue significantly increases reaction time and accident risk. Avoid late-night driving if possible.

Avoid drinking the night before

Even small amounts of alcohol can impair judgment and reaction time. Allow at least 12 hours between drinking and driving to ensure sobriety.

Plan your route and rest stops

Know your route in advance, including fuel stations and safe places to stop. Plan breaks every 2–3 hours to stay alert and refreshed.

Check weather and fire warnings

Before leaving, monitor forecasts for snow, rain, or strong winds. Avoid driving in extreme weather, and follow local alerts for fire or flood warnings.

Follow road safety rules

Observe speed limits, wear seat belts, use indicators, and respect traffic signs. Safe driving reduces the risk of accidents, especially in mountainous areas.

Drive carefully during emergencies

If an accident, vehicle malfunction, or sudden hazard occurs, slow down, stay calm, use hazard lights, and pull over safely. Know basic first aid and emergency contacts.

Be prepared for accidents

Keep calm, move to safety, use hazard lights, have a first aid kit, and report the accident if possible.

Drive safely in snow

Use snow chains or winter tires, drive slowly, keep a shovel and blankets, avoid sudden braking.

Be cautious during strong winds

Reduce speed, hold the wheel firmly, and watch for falling branches or debris.

Stay warm in extreme cold

Dress in layers, keep blankets, carry hot drinks, maintain fuel, and avoid long exposure outside.

Plan for no network coverage

Inform someone of your route, use offline maps, carry flares or reflective triangles, and travel with a companion.

Mountain road precautions

Reduce speed on curves, use engine braking downhill, stay alert for wildlife and debris.

Keep an emergency kit

Include first aid, flashlight, jumper cables, blankets, non-perishable food, water, and a power bank.
